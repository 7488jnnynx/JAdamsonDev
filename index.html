Joy Adamson

Mother of Lions

Here's a time line of Joy's life:

  • 1910 - Born Friederike Victoria Gessner in Troppau, Austrian Silesia, Austria-Hungary (now Opava, Czech Republic)
  • 1920 - Moved to Vienna to live with her Grandmother
  • 1935 - Married an Austrian, Viktor von Klarwill
  • 1937 - Divorced Viktor and moved to Kenya, where she showed a gift for painting flora, earning a position on an expedition to the Chyullu mountain range where she met Peter Bally, a botanist
  • 1938 - Married Peter Bally, who nicknamed her "Joy"
  • 1940 - Met George Adamson, a senior wildlife warden, whilst on Safari
  • 1944 - Divorced Peter and married George, settling down in Kenya
  • 1947 - Began a series of paintings to record tribal life and customs
  • 1956 - Came into care of the lion cub "Elsa", after George shot and killed the cubs mother when he was charged by the lioness
  • 1959 - Joy and her husband began efforts to reintroduce Elsa into the wild and achieved success
  • 1960 - Joy published "Born Free: a lioness of two worlds", a book about Elsa's reintroduction, and it was hugely popular
  • 1961 - Elsa died of tick fever, devastating Joy. Joy published "Living Free: The Story of Elsa and Her Cubs" and "Elsa: The Story of a Lioness"
  • 1962 - Joy published "Forever Free: Elsaâ€™s Pride", recounting Elsa's whole life. The entire Elsa trilogy were best sellers
  • 1963 - Joy founded the Elsa Conservation Trust, an organization committed to wildlife conservation
  • 1969 - "The Spotted Sphinx" was published, marking the moment Joy switches to recounting the tales of her Cheetah Pippa
  • 1970 - Joy's "Pippa: The Cheetah and her Cubs" was published
  • 1972 - Joy published a book of paintings "Joy Adamson's Africa" as well as "Pippa's Challenge"
  • 1975 - Veering from her usual topic of African wildlife, Joy Adamson published "Peoples of Kenya"
  • 1976 - Joy acquired her dream cat, an african leopard she called Penny, as an eight week old cub from a ranger acquaintance of George Adamson's
  • 1978 - The Searching Spirit: Joy Adamson's Autobiography was published
  • 1980 - Joy Admason was mudrered in Shaba National Reserve, in Kenya by a disgruntled employee. However her final books "Queen of Shaba: The Story of an African Leopard" about Penny herself and "Friends from the Forest" were still published that year giving her a total authorship of 12 novels

"Wildlife is something which man cannot construct. Once it is gone, it is gone forever. Man can rebuild a pyramid, but he can't rebuild ecology, or a giraffe."

-- Joy Adamson
